Image Quality Guide

  • Proper Focus: Ensure the lesion is sharply focused and not blurry.
  • Good Lighting: Use natural daylight or a bright white lamp. Avoid harsh flash reflection.
  • Framing: Center the mole in the middle of the photograph.
  • Perspective: Take the photo directly from above (perpendicularly), not at an angle.

Disease Info

Types of Skin Lesions

Understanding the classification categories commonly analyzed by clinical neural networks.

Melanoma

The most serious form of skin cancer, developing in cells called melanocytes. Characterized by irregular margins, multi-colored areas, and asymmetrical structure.

Basal Cell Carcinoma

A slow-growing cancer that originates in the outer layer of the skin. Often appears as a pearly, waxy bump or a flat, flesh-colored lesion.

Nevus / Benign Lesion

Common benign skin growths, commonly referred to as moles. Uniformly circular, symmetrical, and consistent brown/tan pigmentation.

Benign Keratosis-like Lesion

Non-cancerous skin growths that commonly appear in older adults. They are typically brown, black, or light tan, and can have a waxy or scaly surface.

Actinic Keratosis

Pre-cancerous, rough, scaly patches that develop on sun-damaged skin. Often found on the face, lips, ears, back of the hands, or forearms.

Vascular Lesion

Harmless skin marks formed by clusters of blood vessels close to the surface, such as cherry angiomas, angiokeratomas, or pyogenic granulomas.
